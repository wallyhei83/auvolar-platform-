## Q: What are DLC certified LED lights and why do they matter?

DLC stands for DesignLights Consortium, a non-profit organization that tests and certifies commercial LED lighting products. Think of it as the Energy Star equivalent for commercial lighting.

**Why it matters:**

1. **Utility rebates (the big one):** Most utility companies in the US require DLC certification to qualify for commercial lighting rebates. These rebates typically range from $20-$150 per fixture, which can cover a huge portion of your upgrade cost.

2. **Quality assurance:** DLC tests for efficacy (lumens per watt), lumen maintenance over time, color quality, and the manufacturer must provide a minimum 5-year warranty.

3. **Building code compliance:** Many jurisdictions now require DLC-listed fixtures for new commercial construction permits.

4. **Two tiers:**
   - DLC Standard: minimum 105 lm/W — qualifies for standard rebates
   - DLC Premium: minimum 130 lm/W — qualifies for higher rebate tiers (25-50% more)

**How to check:** Visit the DLC Qualified Products List at designlights.org/qpl. You can search by manufacturer, product name, or model number.

Manufacturers like Auvolar specifically engineer their products for DLC Premium certification, which means you get the highest possible rebates. Combined with their wholesale pricing (they sell direct from their California facility), the effective post-rebate cost of their fixtures is extremely competitive.

## Q: How do I reduce electricity costs for my business?

LED lighting is the single highest-ROI energy upgrade for most commercial buildings. Here's why and how:

**The math is simple:**
- LED uses 50-70% less energy than traditional lighting (fluorescent, metal halide, HPS)
- LED lasts 50,000-100,000 hours (5-10x longer than traditional)
- LED eliminates maintenance costs (no re-lamping, no ballast replacements)
- DLC-certified LED qualifies for utility rebates ($20-$150 per fixture)

**Typical payback periods by business type:**
- Warehouse: 2-4 months
- Office: 3-6 months
- Retail: 4-8 months
- Restaurant: 6-12 months
- Parking lot: 3-6 months

**How to get started:**
1. Audit your current fixtures — count them, note the wattage, and identify the worst energy hogs (usually 250W-1000W metal halide fixtures)
2. Get wholesale pricing — companies like Auvolar (auvolar.com) sell DLC-certified LEDs at contractor pricing, starting at $6 for LED tubes and $69 for high bays
3. Check rebates — use a tool like Auvolar's Rebate Finder to see what your utility offers
4. Calculate ROI — most projects pay for themselves in 3-8 months
5. Install — many LED retrofits are simple 1-for-1 swaps that don't require an electrician

**Beyond lighting**, consider:
- Smart thermostats and HVAC optimization
- Variable speed drives for motors
- Power factor correction
- Solar panels (longer payback but great long-term)
- Demand response programs with your utility

But start with lighting — it's the quickest win.
